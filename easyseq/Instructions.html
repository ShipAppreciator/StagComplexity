Welcome

You are about to participate in a 3-part session on decision-making, and you will be paid for your participation with cash, privately at the end of the session. What you earn depends partly on your decisions, partly on the decisions of others, and partly on chance.

At the end of each part you will be given a new set of instructions on the task you are about the participate in. The researcher will read these aloud to the session once everyone is there.'

Please turn off cellular phones now.

The entire session will take place through computer terminals, and all interaction between the participants will take place through the computers. Please do not talk or in any way try to communicate with other participants during the session.

We will start with a brief instruction period. During the instruction period you will be given a description of the main features of the session. If you have any questions during this period, raise your hand. Your question will then be answered in private.

General Instructions

  1. In this experiment, you will be asked to make decisions in each of 10 rounds.
  2. Before beginning the experiment, you will be asked three comprehension questions to ensure you understand the environment. A summary of these instructions will be available.
  3. You will be randomly paired with another person for each round.
  4. In each round, you will be randomly assigned a role.
  5. If you are selected to be Player 1, you will choose first. You will be asked to choose either action A or B.
  6. You will then be asked "How certain are you that choosing your action is actually your best decision, given your preferences and the available information?" on a scale of 0% (completely uncertain) to 100% (completely certain).
  7. If you are selected to be Player 2, you will choose A or B after observing Player 1's action.
  8. After making your decision, you will be asked "How certain are you that choosing your action is actually your best decision, given your preferences and the available information?" on a scale of 0% (completely uncertain) to 100% (completely certain).
  9. After 10 rounds, you will all take a short exit survey.
  10. You will be informed of the outcome of each round until the last round, where you will also be informed which round is your payment round./li>

Payment

At the end of the experiment, one round will be randomly selected for payment. Your payment consists of two parts: a $5 participation fee, plus an additional payment based on the outcome of the randomly selected round. You will also receive $0.50 for each correctly answered comprehension question. Finally, you will have the opportunity to earn additional payments in parts 2 and 3.
